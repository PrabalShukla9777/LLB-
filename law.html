PAPER I: LAW OF CONTRACT – I

▼

Foundation of Legal Agreements

This subject forms the bedrock of commercial law and daily transactions. You'll learn how promises become legally enforceable.

Key Insight: Contract law balances freedom to contract with fairness - ensuring agreements are made willingly and honored properly.

Topics to Master:

  • History and nature of contractual obligation
  • Agreement and Contract: Proposal and acceptance
  • Capacity to contract and Free consent
  • Factors affecting consent: Coercion, Undue Influence
  • Misrepresentation, Fraud, Mistake
  • Void, Voidable, and Unlawful Agreements
  • Government Contracts and Standard Form Contracts
  • Discharge of Contract and its various modes
  • Quasi-Contracts
  • Remedies in Contractual Relations
  • Specific relief: Performance, Rescission, Injunction
Paper Progress:
0%

Recommended Reading

  • Anson's Law of Contract (27th ed. 1998)
  • P.S. Atiya, Introduction to the Law of Contract
  • Avtar Singh, Law of Contract (2000)
  • G.C. Cheshire, H.S. Fifoot and M.P. Fumston, Law of Contract

PAPER II: CONSTITUTIONAL LAW – I

▼

The Framework of Our Democracy

Understand the supreme law of India that defines government structure, citizens' rights, and the relationship between different entities.

Key Insight: The Constitution is not just a legal document but a living instrument that evolves with society while protecting its core values.

Topics to Explore:

  • Meaning and nature of Constitutional Law
  • Historical background of Indian Constitution
  • Essential features and Preamble
  • Separation of powers
  • Union & State Executive
  • Union & State Judiciary
  • Union & State Legislature
  • Concept of Federalism
  • Emergency Provisions
  • Fundamental Rights and Judicial Review
  • Doctrines: Waiver, Eclipse, Severability
Paper Progress:
0%

Recommended Reading

  • Dr. M.D. Chaturvedi: Bharat Ka Samvidhan
  • Dr. J.N. Pandey: Bharat Ka Samvidhan
  • D.D. Basu: Constitution of India (Shorter)
  • Dr. V.N. Shukla: Constitution of India

PAPER III: TORTS & CONSUMER PROTECTION

▼

Civil Wrongs and Consumer Justice

Learn how the law provides remedies for civil wrongs not arising from contracts and protects consumers in the marketplace.

Key Insight: Tort law is about restoring balance - when someone causes harm, the law provides compensation to make things right again.

Topics to Discover:

  • Evolution and definition of Law of Torts
  • Principles of Liability: Fault and No-fault liability
  • Defenses: Consent, Necessity, Private defence
  • Extinguishment of liability
  • Standing: Who may sue and who may not be sued
  • Doctrine of sovereign immunity
  • Vicarious Liability
  • Torts against persons: Assault, battery, defamation
  • Torts affecting property
  • Negligence, Nuisance, and Strict liability
  • Remedies: Damages, Injunction
  • Consumer Protection Act 1986: Rights and remedies
Paper Progress:
0%

Recommended Reading

  • Salmond and Heuston, On the Law of Torts
  • D.D. Basu, The Law of Torts
  • Winfield and Jobowiz on Tort
  • Saraf, D.N., Law of Consumer Protection in India

PAPER IV: FAMILY LAW – I

▼

Law of Personal Relationships

Explore how law regulates the most fundamental human institutions - marriage, family, and personal relationships across different communities.

Key Insight: Family law balances tradition with modernity, respecting religious practices while ensuring justice and equality for all family members.

Topics to Understand:

  • Sources of Hindu Law and Muslim Law
  • Marriage amongst Hindus and Muslims
  • Matrimonial remedies: Restitution, Separation, Nullity, Divorce
  • Minority and Guardianship
  • Maintenance rights
  • Adoption amongst Hindus
  • Acknowledgement of Paternity under Muslim Law
  • Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961
Paper Progress:
0%

Recommended Reading

  • Hindu Marriage Act, 1955
  • Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956
  • Special Marriage Act, 1954
  • D.F. Mulla, Hindu Law
  • Mulla on Muslim Law

PAPER V (B): HUMAN RIGHTS LAW

▼

Defending Human Dignity

Study the legal framework that protects the inherent dignity and equal rights of all human beings, both nationally and internationally.

Key Insight: Human rights are not granted by the state but are inherent to every person - the law's role is to recognize and protect them.

Topics to Champion:

  • Concept, origin and development of Human Rights
  • Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles in Indian Constitution
  • Human Rights of vulnerable groups: Women, Children, Disabled, Tribals, Minorities
  • Fundamental Duties
  • Enforcement of Human Rights in International Law
  • International Humanitarian Law in Armed Conflicts
  • Protection of Civilians and Prisoners of War
  • Refugee Law: Rights and protection

0%

Recommended Reading

  • S.K. Avasthi and R.P. Kataria, Law Relating to Human Rights
  • S.K. Verma, Public International Law
  • Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993
  • Nirmal, B.C. (ed.), Human Rights in India

▼

The Language of Law

Master the linguistic skills essential for legal practice - from drafting documents to arguing cases with precision and clarity.

Key Insight: In law, words are your primary tools - precise language can win cases, while ambiguity can lose them.

Topics to Perfect:

  • Formation of Sentences: Simple, Complex, Compound
  • Legal terminology: Latin maxims and phrases
  • Abbreviations and One Word Substitutions
  • Commercial Letter Writing
  • Composition Skills: Paragraph and Precis writing
  • Legal Essay Writing on contemporary issues
  • Translation between Hindi and English
